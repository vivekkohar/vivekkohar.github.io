I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Applied Chaos Lab , North Carolina State University, Raleigh, NC, USA. I have a doctoral degree in Physics and undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communications Engineering.

Primarily, my research centers around utilizing nonlinear tools and techniques to explore nature and solve engineering challenges. I have worked or presently working on various topics including Chaos Computing and Logical Stochastic Resonance, Noise Induced Phenomenon, Stellar variability and RR Lyrae stars, Synchronization and stability analysis of Complex Networks, heart rate variability, Machine learning techniques including Deep neural networks and compressive sensing. I am also collaborating with SAMSI year long program on Statistical, Mathematical and Computational Methods for Astronomy. You can read more about my research here.

I am very interested in hands on table top experiments using electronics and LASERs. Such experiments are not only valuable for demonstrating research concepts but also serve as an effective tool to communicate the research to wider audience. Some computer generated graphical demonstrations for research concepts can be found at Wolfram Demonstations Project . You need mathematica to create these demonstrations but the end user needs only the freely available CDF Player . Another project worth mentioning is The Scientific Research and Education Network (SciREN) which brings together researchers and K-12 teachers to create lesson plans for school kids.
